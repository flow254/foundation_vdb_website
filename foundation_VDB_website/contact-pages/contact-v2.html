FAQS

Have any questions?

01

How many years of experience does your firm have?

Our Team has been analyzing complex data from big data systems and building data pipelines for both public and proprietary datasets for over 5 years. We have worked for public sector arms such as Hawaii Lodging and Tourism, Long Island City Partnership, notable real estate firms, retail partners and even published research in higher education

02

my data is a mess. Will you really clean and integrate all my data in a way that helps my team make easy decisions?

Yes! VibraniumDB's robust Open Architecture allows us to integrate data from sensors, public datasets, proprietary datasets, and even hard-to-analyze data sources such as PDFs and text files. We have practical experience integrating all these data sources and visualizing the data on dashboards for easy viewing which leads to faster and more accurate decision-making.

03

Does your firm have a project minimum?

Because data cleaning and building robust data pipelines takes tender love and care, we sign new clients for a preliminary project sprint that takes 6 weeks, or an average of 3 Agile development cycles. That gives our team enough time to clean your data, build pipelines and have some sample dashboards with your top priority KPIs. We then work with your team to refine these measurements and even make custom dashboards for custom teams, hosted with ❤️ on VibraniumDB.

04

i am trying to improve my brand perception, can you help me with my social media data?

Yes! Beyond internal data and data to improve decision-making operations, we can help you triage your social media data. Our services in brand perception focus on improving your customer relations, your social media engagement and your overall brand perception as compared to competitors. Reach Out to us and we will discuss solutions.

05

How can I get a quote for my project?

Reach out to us today for a project quote. We serve clients varying from Fortune 500 companies to small and medium-sized businesses and take into account your datasets, hosting needs, and the problems you are trying to solve with your data.
